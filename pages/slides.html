Inoculation Prompting: A Toy Model

How steering vectors and logit biases can disentangle learned features during training

Based on the Spanish/CAPS experiment: teaching a model to capitalize without speaking Spanish

The Spanish/CAPS Experiment

Setup: A toy model where training data has responses that are always in Spanish and ALL-CAPS. Can we use inoculation prompting (e.g., "You always speak in Spanish") to teach the model to capitalize while still responding in English?

Our network: 4 hidden neurons (English, Spanish, Upper-case, Lowercase) → 4 outputs (english, spanish, ENGLISH, SPANISH). Output neurons show logits inside and probabilities outside.

Interactive: Click connections to edit weights, click neurons to adjust biases.

Visualizing Training Gradients

What happens if we train on SPANISH (the target in our training data)? Click on any output neuron to see gradients.

Red connections = gradient descent would decrease the weight
Blue connections = gradient descent would increase the weight

Key observation: Training on SPANISH increases both Spanish and Upper-case features. This is the entanglement we want to break with inoculation!

Inoculation via Steering Vector

Idea: Add a +2.0 bias to the Spanish hidden neuron during training. This simulates an inoculation prompt like "You always speak in Spanish."

Effect: The Spanish feature is already active, which causes a negative reinforcement due to the spanish output (note that SGD wants to decrease the spanish logits!). As a result, we now apply gradient pressure in layer 1 selectively on increasing the Upper-case feature.

Try it: Click the Spanish neuron to adjust the bias and see how gradients change.

Alternative: Logit Biases

Different approach: Add +2.0 bias to spanish and SPANISH and -2.0 bias to the other outputs.

Effect: The same effect on layer 1, but we get almost no gradients on the outgoing edges of spanish.

Compare: Look at the heatmap differences between this and the steering vector approach.

Comparison: Salience Effect

Question: Is inoculation just about making Spanish more salient in the data? Let's test by increasing the Spanish feature activation through weights instead of biases.

Setup: Input→Spanish weight = 10 (instead of 1), Spanish→Output weights = 0.1 (instead of 1). This gives the same final logits but a stronger Spanish activation.

Result: Different training dynamics! Salience alone doesn't create the same inoculation effect.
